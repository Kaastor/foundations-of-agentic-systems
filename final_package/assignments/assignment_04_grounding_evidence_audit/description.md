# Assignment 4: Grounding Evidence Audit

**Linked meeting:** [meeting_plan.md](/home/przemek/Biznes/Kursy/course-generation/understanding/runs/Agentic-GenAI-Systems/final_package/meeting_plan.md)
Meeting 4: “How Does It Act and What Counts as Evidence?”

**Why this assignment exists:** Students need to distinguish grounded claims from outputs that only sound plausible.

**Main reasoning-loop emphasis:** `compare -> critique -> revise`

**Expected time:** `4 hours`

**Coding level:** `bounded code-reading`, with optional workflow rerun

## Brief

Audit one AI-generated or student-generated claim set about the literature-review assistant’s output.

If you want a fixed starting point, use [starter_claim_set.md](/home/przemek/Biznes/Kursy/course-generation/understanding/runs/Agentic-GenAI-Systems/final_package/assignments/assignment_04_grounding_evidence_audit/starter_claim_set.md).

Your task is to decide:

`Which claims are grounded by tool observations and verification artifacts, which are only weakly supported, and what would a better bounded final answer look like?`

You must compare grounded and weakly grounded claims and then revise one final bounded answer or recommendation.
Also inspect one small implementation surface that helps explain why some outputs count as evidence and others do not.

## Submission Checklist

- identify at least two grounded claims
- identify at least two weakly supported or unsupported claims
- cite tool observations and at least one verification artifact
- inspect at least one named source module and explain how it enforces or exposes grounding
- revise one bounded final answer or recommendation
- explain what counts as evidence rather than mere output
